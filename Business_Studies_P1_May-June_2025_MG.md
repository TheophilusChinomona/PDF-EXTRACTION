**2.4** Rights of employers in terms of the Labour Relations Act/LRA (max 6 marks)

- Form employer organisations to represent them in labour related matters.
- Form bargaining councils for collective bargaining purposes.
- Lockout employees who engage in labour action/an unprotected/illegal strike.
- Dismiss employees who engage in unprotected/illegal strike/misconduct such as intimidation/violence during a strike action.
- Not pay/remunerate employees who have participated in a protected strike for services/work they did not do during the strike.

**2.5** Types of defensive strategies (max 6 marks)

> *Marker instruction: NOTE: Mark the first TWO (2) only.*

**Divestiture:**
- Disposing/Selling some assets/divisions that are no longer profitable/productive.
- Selling off divisions/product lines with slow growth potential.
- Decreasing the number of shareholders by selling ownership.
- Paying off debts by selling unproductive assets.
- Withdrawing/Divesting their investment share in another business.

**Retrenchment:**
- Terminating the employment contracts/Letting go of employees for operational/structural/restructuring reasons/reduce costs/expenses.
- Decreasing the number of product lines/Closing certain departments may result in some workers becoming redundant.

**Liquidation:**
- Selling all assets/Bringing the business activities to an end due to the inability to repay creditors/bankruptcy/lack of capital.
- Selling the entire business in order to pay shareholders a fair price for their shares.
- Allowing creditors to apply for forced liquidation in order to have their claims settled.
- Companies in financial distress/difficulty may appeal for business rescue to avoid liquidation.

**2.6.1** Identification of Act (2 marks)

**Compensation for Occupational Injuries and Diseases Amendment Act/COIDA**

---

**2.6.2** Other advantages of COIDA (max 4 marks)

> *Marker instruction: NOTE: 1. Do not award marks for 'monthly contributions to a common fund to protect workers in the event of workplace accidents'. 2. Accept relevant facts if the Act was incorrectly identified.*

- Promotes health and safety of employees which reduces accidents in the workplace.
- Employees do not contribute towards this fund, which improves morale in the workplace.
- Claiming processes are relatively simple, resulting in timeous pay-outs.
- Eliminates time and costs spent on lengthy civil court proceedings.
- Any compensation to an employee/the family is exempt from income tax.
- Employers are protected from financial burden should an accident occur in the workplace provided that the employer was not negligent.
- Makes businesses more socially responsible.
- Workers are treated with dignity and respect as businesses view them as valuable assets.
- Covers all employees at the workplace if both parties meet all the necessary safety provisions in the Act.
- Creates a framework for acceptable employment practices and safety regulations.
- Employees are compensated financially for any injury/disability resulting from performing their duties.
- In the event of death of an employee, his/her dependent(s) will receive financial support.
- Employees receive medical assistance provided there is no other medical assistance option.
- Medical expenses/Other types of compensation are paid depending on the type/severity of the injuries.

**2.7** Strategic management process (max 6 marks)

**Option 1:**
- Have a clear vision/mission statement/measurable/realistic objective in place.
- Identify opportunities/weaknesses/strengths/threats by conducting environmental scanning/situational analysis.
- Tools available: SWOT analysis/Porter's Five Forces model/PESTLE analysis/industrial analysis tools.
- Formulate alternative strategies to respond to the challenges.
- Develop action plan(s), including tasks/deadlines/resources.
- Implement selected strategies by communicating to all stakeholders/organising resources/motivating staff.
- Continuously evaluate/monitor/measure strategies to take corrective action.

**Option 2:**
- Review/Analyse/Re-examine their vision/mission statement.
- Conduct environmental analysis using SWOT/PESTLE/Porter's Five Forces.
- Formulate a strategy, such as a defensive/retrenchment strategy.
- Implement a strategy, using a template such as an action plan.
- Control/Evaluate/Monitor the implemented strategy to identify gaps/deviations.
- Take corrective action to ensure goals/objectives are met.

> *Marker instruction: NOTE: The procedure may be in any order.*

- Sort received documents/CVs according to assessment/selection criteria.

- Reference checks/Vetting process to verify content of CVs.
- Compile a shortlist of potential candidates.

- Invite shortlisted candidates for an interview.
- A written offer is made to the selected candidate.
- Inform unsuccessful applicants about the outcome.

**Option 2:**
- Receive documentation and sort according to criteria.
- Evaluate CVs and create a shortlist/screen applicants.
- Check information in CVs and contact references.
- Conduct preliminary sifting interviews.
- Assess/Test candidates for senior positions.
- Conduct interviews with shortlisted candidates.
- Offer employment in writing to the selected candidate(s).

### QUESTION 5: BUSINESS ENVIRONMENTS (LEGISLATION) — Consumer Protection Act (40 marks)

**Introduction:**
- The CPA aims to protect consumers from exploitation and unfair business practices.
- Introduced to promote equality in the consumer market.
- Businesses have a legal responsibility to protect/promote consumer rights.
- Businesses that comply can reduce heavy penalties.

**Purpose of the CPA:**
- Promotes fair/accessible and sustainable marketplaces.
- Establishes national standards to protect consumers regardless of economic status.
- Provides guidelines for better consumer information and prohibits unfair practices.
- Promotes responsible consumer behaviour.
- Promotes consistent laws relating to consumer transactions.
- Establishes a National Consumer Commission (NCC).
- Ensures consumers have access to information for informed choices.
- Promotes rights of historically disadvantaged individuals as consumers.
- Ensures consumers are not misled/deceived by suppliers.
- Promotes consumer safety from hazardous products/services.
- Strengthens a culture of consumer rights and responsibilities.
- Empowers consumers to take legal action if rights not upheld.
- Protects consumers against unfair contract terms.
- Protects consumers against unscrupulous businesses/fly-by-night franchisors.
- Allows for fair/effective dispute resolution.

**Impact of the CPA on businesses:**

*Positives:*
- Safeguarded from dishonest competitors.
- Protected if regarded as consumers.
- Prevents larger businesses from undermining smaller ones.
- May gain consumer loyalty if they comply.
- Enables dispute resolution through NCC/Consumer Court/Industrial ombudsmen.
- May build a good image if they do not violate consumer rights.

*Negatives:*
- Confidential business information may become available to competitors.
- Penalties for non-compliance may be very high.
- May feel unnecessarily burdened by legal processes.
- Have to disclose more information about products/processes/services.
- Processes and procedures can be expensive/time consuming.
- Consumers can take advantage and return goods unnecessarily.
- Staff training/Legal experts increase operational costs.
- Business documents need to be simplified/revamped at extra cost.
- Administration costs increase for plain language contracts.
- May need insurance against consumer claims.
- Must replace/repair faulty items/refund within six months.
- Supply chain management must change for defective goods replacement.
- IT systems need improvement for detailed records/NCC reporting.

**Consumer rights in terms of the CPA:**

| Right | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| Right to choose | Choose suppliers; shop around; return unsafe/defective goods; reject goods not matching sample; cancel/renew fixed term agreements; request written quotations |
| Right to privacy | Stop/restrict unwanted direct marketing; object to promotional emails/telesales; stop sharing of personal details |
| Right to fair and honest dealings | No physical force/harassment; no misleading/false information; no pyramid schemes; no overbooking/overselling |
| Right to disclosure and information | Plain language contracts; fully inclusive prices; correct labelling; unit and bulk pricing; lower price if two displayed; country of origin/expiry/ingredients disclosed |
| Right to fair and responsible marketing | No misleading pricing/benefits; 5-day cooling-off period for direct marketing |
| Right to fair value/good quality and safety | Demand quality goods/services; implied/written warranty; hazardous product guidance; reject non-matching samples; return faulty items within 6 months |
| Right to accountability by suppliers | Protection in lay-bye agreements; honour credit vouchers/prepaid services |
| Right to fair terms and conditions | Written notices of limiting clauses; no unfair prices |
| Right to equality in the consumer market | No limiting access; no varying quality; no discrimination in marketing; no different prices for same goods |

**Ways businesses can comply with the CPA:**
- Disclose prices of all products on sale.
- Provide adequate training to staff on the CPA.
- All agreements must provide for a five-day cooling-off period.
- Ensure goods/services are standardised/same quality.
- Comply with promotional competition requirements.
- Comply with label/packaging information display requirements.
- Display business name on all business documents.
- Bundling should benefit consumers.
- Implement complaint-facilitation measures (e.g. suggestion boxes).

**Conclusion:**
- The CPA requires both businesses and consumers to act responsibly.
- Correct implementation enables businesses to gain positive impact and reduce negative impact.
- Applying consumer rights increases market share, gains loyal customers, and improves customer base.
- Compliance strengthens overall market integrity by ensuring balanced relationships.

### QUESTION 6: BUSINESS OPERATIONS (HUMAN RESOURCES FUNCTION) — Essay (40 marks)

**Introduction:**
- Recruitment procedure is a systematic process for identifying and filling vacant positions.
- External recruitment enables businesses to employ qualified people from outside.
- The interviewer is responsible for administration and logistical arrangements.
- Reasons for termination should be clearly explained to all employees.

**Recruitment procedure as a human resources activity:**
- Evaluate the job/prepare a job analysis (job specification/job description).
- Prepare the job description to determine responsibilities/key performance areas.
- Indicate the job specification/description to attract suitable candidates.
- Choose method of recruitment (internal/external).
- Vacancies can be internally advertised via email/word of mouth/posters/staff notices.
- If internal recruitment unsuccessful, consider external recruitment.
- Select relevant recruitment sources (agencies/tertiary institutions/newspapers).
- Prepare advertisement with relevant information (company name/contact details/contact person).
- Place advertisement in appropriate media.

**Impact of external recruitment on businesses:**

*Positives:*
- New candidates bring new talents/ideas/experiences/skills.
- Larger pool of candidates to choose from.
- Helps meet affirmative action and BBBEE targets.
- Minimises unhappiness/conflict amongst current employees.
- Better chance of getting suitable candidate who doesn't need much training.

*Negatives:*
- Information on CVs/from referees may not be reliable.
- Many unsuitable applications slow down selection process.
- New candidates take longer to adjust to new work environment.
- External sources can be expensive (agencies/advertisements).
- Selection process may not be effective — incompetent candidate may be chosen.
- Recruitment process takes longer due to background checks.
- In-service training may be needed, decreasing productivity.
- May limit promotion/growth opportunities for existing employees.

**Role of the interviewer during the interview:**
- Allocate same amount of time to each candidate.
- Introduce members of the interviewing panel to each candidate.
- Make the interviewee feel at ease.
- Explain the purpose of the interview.
- Record interviewees' responses for future reference.
- Do not mislead the interviewee with unrelated questions.
- Avoid discriminatory/controversial questions.
- Provide opportunity for interviewee to ask questions.
- Close interview by thanking the interviewee.

**Reasons for termination of an employment contract:**
- Employer may dismiss for valid reasons (unsatisfactory performance/misconduct).
- Employer may no longer have work for redundant employees/restructuring.
- Employer may retrench employees due to insolvency.
- Employees decided to leave/resign voluntarily for better opportunities.
- Employee reached pre-determined retirement age.
- Incapacity to work due to illness/injuries.
- By mutual agreement between employer and employee.
- Duration of employment contract expires/comes to an end.

**Conclusion:**
- Effective recruitment procedures help achieve recruitment goals.
- External recruitment ensures a diversified workforce.
- Interviewers who understand their role conduct effective interviews.
- HR managers should be knowledgeable on termination reasons to avoid lawsuits.
