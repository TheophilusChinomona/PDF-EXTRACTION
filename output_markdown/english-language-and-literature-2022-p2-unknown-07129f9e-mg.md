## Section A

### Question 1 [32 marks] (Poetry Analysis)

**Explore how William Blake presents ideas and attitudes towards the poverty and suffering of children in ‘Holy Thursday’ (E) and make connections with one or two other poems from your collection.**

> **Marker instruction:** A higher level response (levels 4 to 6) will: AO2 Use poetic and stylistic analysis effectively to support a coherent interpretation; AO1 Use vocabulary and terminology effectively; AO3 Make telling use of relevant literary or other contexts; AO4 Make interesting and illuminating use of points of connection between poems. A lower level response (levels 1 to 3) will: AO2 Identify some examples of poetic and stylistic techniques; AO1 Use some terminology appropriately; AO3 Make some relevant but limited use of contexts; AO4 Make a few relevant points of connection.

**Introduction:**
- The indicative content shows an integrated approach to AO2 and AO1 with additional guidance for AO3 and AO4.

**Body:**

*Poetic and stylistic techniques (AO2 and AO1)*

*Context (AO3)*
  - Of the poems: 'Songs of Experience' poem foregrounding the bleak world and criticising institutions.
  - Of the wider literary/cultural: Social commentary on the plight of the poorest in society and corrupt institutions exploiters.

*Connections (AO4)*
  - Possible connections: 'Holy Thursday (I)', 'London', and 'Chimney Sweeper (E)' regarding presentation of creative processes and social conditions.
